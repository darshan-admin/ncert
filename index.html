Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What is the difference between NCERT and CBSE Books?

CBSE or Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) is on the board of education while the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) is the authority who is responsible for publishing the textbooks. Therefore, NCERT publishes the textbooks which are used in the CBSE board schools for the students.

Are NCERT books enough for entrance level examinations?

The books by NCERT act as good references for exam preparation but not sufficient. These books will definitely help you in understanding the core concepts of various subjects, but these will not help in advance problems. Hence, students shall refer to reference books to understand the higher difficulty level’s problems. Apart from the books, sample papers and previous years question papers are absolutely helpful for entrance level examinations like JEE Main, Olympiad, NEET etc.

Why should NCERT books be taken seriously?

NCERT books are extremely important and beneficial for the students at the same time because they are the source of many question papers for various exams. If you take a closer look at the previous years’ papers of the board exam or any entrance exams, you will definitely understand the frequency of the usage of these NCERT textbooks there. Thus, these books shall be taken seriously as they help to understand various topics with in-depth knowledge and solve the exercise questions or sample papers effortlessly. It also helps to prepare for many entrance exams such as NEET, JEE Main etc.

How to read these books more effectively?

  • To get a good understanding of each topic, students must read the textbooks thoroughly and jot down the important points for quick reference.
  • Students must solve all the questions from the textbook exercises so that it becomes easy to understand all the complex concepts and formulas from an individual subject.
  • Practicing previous year questions papers and sample papers benefits the students to increase their speed of solving problems with a high accuracy rate. Hence they will become more efficient before the exam.
  • Good practice of drawing diagrams or flowcharts for a better understanding of the topics help to achieve a good score in the exam as well.
